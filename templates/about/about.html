About Faamaya EcoTech

The Eco-social Restoration Platform aims to contribute to the transformation of the Great Green Wall initiative by combining AI-driven species selection with deep community engagement. While current tree planting sees 70–80% failure rates across the Sahel, our integrated approach leverages environmental DNA analysis, satellite imaging, machine learning, and community-owned monitoring to create self-sustaining ecosystems. Our five-year implementation plan transitions from data collection to local ownership, generating revenue through carbon credits while empowering communities via mobile payment systems and AR tools. Read our publications to explore how we turn reforestation into a self-learning, income-generating ecosystem.

The Challenge We Are Solving

The Great Green Wall initiative has invested billions, yet faces staggering 70–80% tree planting failure rates across the Sahel region. Only 4% of the original reforestation goals have been achieved. This isn’t just an environmental crisis—it is a failure of approach that impacts millions of lives.

Our Innovation: Where AI Meets Community

We are building a groundbreaking platform that combines cutting-edge AI with community-driven implementation to transform ecological restoration:

  • AI-Driven Intelligence: Environmental DNA analysis, remote sensing, ML, and portable soil testing recommend the best species for each location.
  • Community Empowerment: Tools like AR visualization, IoT monitoring, and mobile payment systems provide economic incentives and ownership to local people.

Why Join Us Now

  1. Technological Breakthrough: Our AI is tailored for the Sahel, using botanical, geological, and social data to boost reforestation success rates.
  2. Massive Impact Potential: Our 5-year plan aims to revolutionize tree planting, fight climate change, and enhance livelihoods.
  3. Strong Team: We blend expertise in AI, environmental science, and community development—rooted in deep local knowledge.

What Makes Us Different

  • Self-improving data systems that learn from results
  • Community ownership and knowledge independence
  • Revenue generation via carbon credits
  • Reforestation as a social movement—not just a technical fix
